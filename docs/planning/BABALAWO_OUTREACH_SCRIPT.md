## Tips for Success

### DO:
✅ Research each Babalawo before reaching out (personalize when possible)
✅ Use proper greetings (Àbọrú Àbọyè Àbọṣíṣẹ)
✅ Be respectful of their time
✅ Emphasize the pilot/experimental nature
✅ Highlight how it serves the community, not just business
✅ Offer a demo/call rather than asking for immediate commitment
✅ Follow up once, then respect their silence

### DON'T:
❌ Send generic copy-paste messages
❌ Be overly salesy or pushy
❌ Disrespect the tradition by treating it as just a "business opportunity"
❌ Spam multiple messages
❌ Make promises you can't keep
❌ Use informal language or emojis excessively

---

## Recommended Approach

1. **Start with Version 1 or 4** (balanced and professional)
2. **Personalize** with something specific about their work
3. **Send during respectful hours** (avoid late night/early morning)
4. **Be patient** - many Babalawos are not extremely active on social media
5. **Prepare a demo** - have the platform ready to show
6. **Create a simple one-pager** with screenshots they can review

---

## Sample Personalization

Instead of:
> "Baba [Name], I hope this message finds you well..."

Try:
> "Baba [Name], I recently watched your video on [specific topic] and was moved by how clearly you explained [concept]. I hope this message finds you well..."

This shows genuine interest and respect.

---

## Platform Demo Preparation

When they respond positively, be ready to:
1. **Schedule a 15-20 minute video call** (Zoom/WhatsApp)
2. **Show them their potential profile** (create a mock one beforehand)
3. **Walk through the booking flow** from a client's perspective
4. **Demonstrate the payment system**
5. **Ask for their specific needs** and take notes
6. **Set clear expectations** about the pilot timeline
